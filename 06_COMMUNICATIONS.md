## GROUND RULES

1. **Dylan only.** Every SMS and every email starts and ends as Dylan. Never break this persona.
2. **TCPA opt-out in every first message.** Every DYLAN_INTRO includes "Reply STOP to opt out."
3. **No emojis in SMS.** Documents use them, agent-sent SMS does not.
4. **No all-caps.** Comes across as shouting.
5. **Under 160 characters for automated messages.** Avoid carrier concatenation; some don't render split messages cleanly.
6. **Real names only.** Always use the candidate's first name from the CSV — never "there" or generic greetings.

## MESSAGE 4 — RE-ENGAGEMENT (CONTEXT-AWARE)
**Fires:** Step 1 (Re-engage Stalled) for stalled contacts (96+ hours silent)  
**Generated by:** Claude Haiku (claude-haiku-4-5-20251001) — no fixed template  
**Constraint:** < 160 characters, no emoji, personalised to actual conversation state

**Examples by situation:**

| Situation detected | Example re-engagement |

| Candidate said they'd "get back" | "Hi Tony, no rush — just wanted to check if you're still interested in CNA positions across NYC." |

**System will SKIP (return None) if:**
- Active email thread in the last 24 hours (no need to double-contact)
- Interview or appointment is scheduled (don't interrupt)
- Documents submitted in last 4 days (mid-process, let it proceed)
- Contact was messaged within last 24 hours for any reason

## EMAIL RESPONSES — GENERAL INQUIRY (NEW, v2.0)

**Generated by:** Claude Opus (claude-opus-4-5) in `email_context_bridge.py`
**Fires:** Any unread email without document attachments
**Medium:** Gmail Draft (Aditya reviews and sends manually — NEVER auto-sent, same as document review)
**No fixed template** — unlike DYLAN_INTRO or DOCUMENT_CHECKLIST_MSG, this is
drafted fresh per email based on that specific candidate's Gmail + Quo history.

**Voice rules the model is given (same Dylan persona, email register):**
- Warm, direct, professional — never generic if history exists
- Reference what was actually said or sent — never repeat a full document
  list that was already sent in full
- No emojis, no exclamation spam, no "I hope this email finds you well"
- Sign off as Dylan, no last name, no title

**Example of what a good draft looks like (illustrative, not a template to paste):**
```
Subject: Re: Question about shifts

Hi Maria,

Good question — we currently have AM and PM shifts open in Brooklyn for
CNAs, and based on what you mentioned last week you're open to either.
Rates are $22-26/hr depending on the facility.

Let me know which works better for you and I'll get you connected.

Dylan
BlueLine Staffing
info@bluelinestaffing.com
```

**Before this fires, three other outcomes are possible instead of a draft —
see 05_PIPELINE_REFERENCE.md STEP 5 for the full decision tree:**
- `SKIP` — nothing needs a reply (duplicate, automated notice, etc.)
- `FLAG_HUMAN` — appended to `master_needs_human_review_email.txt`, no draft
  written (opt-out language, legal/pricing content, client-originated email,
  or anything the model wasn't confident about)

## QUO CONTACT NAMES — THE NAMING RULE

This is not a message, but it governs how every contact appears in Quo. **Never deviate.**

```
firstName = "Full Legal Name"         Examples: "Jane Doe", "Maria Santos"
lastName  = "LICENSE, Borough"        Examples: "RN, Brooklyn", "CNA, Bronx"

Opt-out:
firstName = "DO NOT MESSAGE - Full Legal Name"
lastName  = ""  (cleared)
```

**Why this matters:**
- Recruiter can see at a glance: who is this nurse, what license, what borough
- Re-engagement messages pull license type from lastName field
- "DO NOT MESSAGE" prefix is how the system identifies opted-out contacts even after a restart
